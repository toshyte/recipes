Creamy Garlic Tuscan Shrimp

April 7, 2026 20 Minutes 4 Servings

There are certain recipes that earn permanent spots in our weeknight rotation, and this creamy garlic Tuscan shrimp is one of them. Everything happens in a single pan, it takes barely 20 minutes from fridge to table, and every time I make it someone asks for the recipe.

Why you'll love this recipe: One pan, minimal prep, restaurant-quality results. The cream sauce practically makes itself while the shrimp cook, and the whole thing comes together faster than delivery.

What Makes This Dish Work

The secret is building layers of flavor without adding complexity. You sear the shrimp first to get those golden edges, then use the same pan — with all those caramelized bits — to build the sauce. The garlic blooms in butter, the cream reduces into something luxurious, and the parmesan ties everything together.

Sun-dried tomatoes add a tangy sweetness that cuts through the richness, and the spinach wilts down in seconds, adding color and freshness. It's the kind of dish that looks like you spent an hour on it.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Getting the Shrimp Right

Pat them dry — seriously, this matters more than any seasoning. Wet shrimp steam instead of sear, and you'll miss out on those caramelized edges that make this dish. Once they're dry, hit them with salt, pepper, and a good pinch of Italian seasoning.

Use a large skillet and don't crowd the pan. If your shrimp are touching, they're steaming each other. Two minutes per side is all you need — they'll finish cooking later in the sauce.

The sauce thickens beautifully in just 3-4 minutes

Building the Sauce

Once the shrimp are out, the pan is primed with flavor. Drop in butter and garlic — 30 seconds is all you need before it turns fragrant. Then in goes the cream, sun-dried tomatoes, and red pepper flakes.

Let the sauce simmer for a few minutes. You'll see it start to coat the back of a spoon — that's when you know it's ready. Stir in the parmesan off the heat so it melts smoothly without getting grainy.

Bringing It All Together

Spinach goes in next — it looks like a mountain but wilts down to almost nothing. Then the shrimp go back in for a quick toss. One minute in the sauce is enough to warm everything through without overcooking the shrimp.

Tips for the Best Results

  • Don't skip drying the shrimp. Paper towels, both sides. This is the difference between golden and rubbery.
  • Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes — they're softer and more flavorful than the dry-packed kind.
  • Add parmesan off the heat. Stirring it into boiling liquid makes it clump. Let the sauce cool for 30 seconds first.
  • Serve immediately. The sauce thickens as it sits. If reheating, add a splash of cream.

What to Serve With It

This shrimp is rich enough to stand on its own, but it's incredible over pasta, rice, or with crusty bread for soaking up the sauce. My go-to pairings:

  • Linguine or penne — the classic move, toss it right in the sauce
  • Garlic bread — because you can never have too much garlic
  • Cauliflower rice — keeps it low carb without sacrificing satisfaction
  • Simple green salad — something fresh and acidic to balance the cream

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • to taste salt and black pepper
  • for garnish fresh basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels and season generously with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning on both sides.
  2. Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook for 2 minutes per side until pink with golden edges. Remove to a plate and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining tablespoon of butter to the same pan. Add minced garlic and saute for 30 seconds until fragrant, scraping up the browned bits from the bottom.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream. Add the sun-dried tomatoes and red pepper flakes. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce begins to thicken.
  5. Remove from heat for 30 seconds, then stir in the grated parmesan until fully melted and smooth. Return to low heat, add the spinach, and stir until just wilted — about 1 minute.
  6. Return the shrimp to the pan and toss gently to coat everything in the sauce. Cook for 1 more minute to warm through. Garnish with fresh basil and serve immediately over pasta, rice, or with crusty bread.

Recipe Notes

  • Storage: Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The sauce thickens when cold — add a splash of cream when reheating over low heat.
  • Substitutions: Use chicken breast (sliced thin) instead of shrimp for a different protein. Coconut cream works as a dairy-free swap, though the flavor will be slightly different.
  • Make it spicier: Add an extra 1/2 tsp of red pepper flakes, or stir in a teaspoon of calabrian chili paste with the garlic.
  • Freeze: Not recommended — cream sauces tend to separate when frozen and reheated.
